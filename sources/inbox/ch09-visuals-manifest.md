All images sourced from Wikimedia Commons only. License verified via the Commons API
(`action=query&prop=imageinfo&iiprop=extmetadata`) before download. Files saved to
`app/public/visuals/` with the `ch09-` prefix. All photos were downloaded at their

## ch09-laser-cutting-sparks.jpg

**What it shows:** A close-up shot of a fiber-laser cutting head mid-cut on a steel
sheet: the focusing lens/nozzle assembly is visible top-center, with the blue-white
laser interaction point at the sheet surface and a bright shower of orange sparks
streaming off to the right. In the background/foreground, small punched-out slugs and
several cut circular holes are visible in the sheet, and the reflection of the cutting
point is visible in the mirror-polished plate. An excellent, unambiguous "laser cutting
in progress, beam and sparks clearly visible" photo — the single best image found for
this topic.

## ch09-laser-cut-nested-parts.jpg

**What it shows:** A wide shop-floor shot of a large CNC gantry cutting table (brand
"TAYOR", labelled on the machine as a combined CNC/Laser/Plasma cutting table) with a
steel plate on the bed showing a clear **nested layout of multiple cut parts** —
various polygonal and round shapes tightly packed together with visible cut lines and
small punched holes, exactly the kind of nesting layout used to minimize scrap. Two
workers stand at the left, one operating a control pendant. **Caveat: the cutting head
and the wet/slag residue on the bed strongly suggest this specific shot is of a
plasma-cutting pass (the machine itself is described in its filename as a combined
"CNC-Laser-Plasma" cutting table), not a confirmed laser cut** — I could not find a
Commons photo of a laser-specific nested sheet layout as clean as this one. Flagging
so the caption can say "CNC-cut nested parts" rather than claim it is laser-specific.

- Original file: `File:2X6m-Steel-Plate-CNC-Laser-Plasma-Cutting-Table .jpg`

- Author: Zgyricky
- License: CC BY-SA 4.0
- Suggested topic: "Laser-cut parts / nested parts on a sheet" (topic 2) — see caveat above
- Type: Photo (original size, 103 KB, no re-encoding needed)

stack of raw sheet stock sits on a blue table in the foreground. **Caveat: the machine

## ch09-laser-cut-edge-kerf.jpg

**What it shows:** A macro close-up of the cut edge of a 12 mm thick S355MC steel
plate after laser cutting, photographed resting on a plywood workbench. The
characteristic fine, near-vertical striation lines (the "kerf" texture / cut-front
striae typical of laser and other thermal cutting processes) are clearly visible
running down the cut face, along with a partially-cut slug/hole in the top face. An
excellent, unambiguous close-up of what a laser-cut edge actually looks like at
thickness.

  steel S355MC, 12.0mm")

## Topics I could NOT find a strong dedicated image for

- **Topic 2, a confirmed-laser (as opposed to plasma/generic CNC) nested-parts-on-a-
  sheet photo.** Searched "laser cut nested parts sheet metal", "sheet metal nesting
  laser", "laser cut parts sheet steel nesting", plus Commons' own "Category:Laser
  cutting" and "Category:Laser-cut products" (the latter is almost entirely laser-cut
  wood/plywood craft and art pieces, not sheet metal). The best sheet-metal nesting
  layout I found (`ch09-laser-cut-nested-parts.jpg`) is captioned by its uploader as a
  combined "CNC-Laser-Plasma" cutting table and visually looks like a plasma pass
  (wet bed, slag) — used with an honest caveat rather than discarded.
- **Topic 5, a genuine photograph (not a CAD rendering) of a finished multi-bend
  sheet-metal bracket or enclosure.** Searched "sheet metal chassis bent bracket",
  "sheet metal enclosure prototype", "metal bracket L shape fabricated",
  "Blechgehäuse", "Schaltschrank Blechgehäuse", "server rack sheet metal frame" — these
  returned either archived book/PDF scans or nothing on-topic. Used the CAD-rendered
  folded box corner (`ch09-box-corner-fold-joint.jpg`) as the closest honest substitute
  — flagged clearly as a rendering, not a photo, in that entry.
- **Topic 3, an action shot of a press brake mid-bend with an operator forming a
  part.** Searched "press brake bending operator sheet", "Blechbiegen Abkantpresse",
  "sheet metal bending worker" and browsed Commons' "Category:Press brakes" (about 30
  files, mostly static product/studio photos of idle machines). Used a real
  workshop-floor photo of an idle-but-real press brake (`ch09-press-brake-machine.jpg`)
  plus a tooling closeup as the closest honest substitutes.

## Notes on quantity

This manifest has 9 files rather than the requested 6-8, because topic 3 (press brake)
and topic 4 (bend allowance / flat pattern) each turned up a strong primary image plus
a genuinely complementary secondary image (tooling closeup; a real box flat-pattern
development alongside the abstract bend-allowance diagram) that seemed worth keeping
rather than discarding.
