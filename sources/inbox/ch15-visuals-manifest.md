## Topics I could NOT find a strong dedicated image for / gaps

- **Topic 3, a zip-tie-style small connector (XT60 or Molex) actually showing a
  visible latch mechanism.** Searched "XT60 connector" (only a schematic sketch
  turned up, not a photo), "Molex connector latch", "locking circular connector".
  Plain `File:Molex female connector.jpg` / `File:Molex male connector.jpg` (both
  CC BY-SA 2.5, verified) exist on Commons but their latch tangs are not clearly
  visible in-frame, so I chose the Veam PowerLock industrial connector instead —
  it unambiguously shows a locking/latching mechanism, at the cost of being a
  higher-current industrial part rather than a small hobby connector. If a
  small-connector-specific image is wanted later, the two Molex files above are
  verified and available.
- **Topic 9, zip ties specifically (as opposed to lacing cord).** Commons'
  `Category:Cable lacing` is rich (this is where `ch15-cable-harness-lacing.jpg`
  came from) but a comparably clean "wire harness with plastic zip ties" photo
  did not surface in searches ("wire harness zip ties", "cable ties harness");
  what did turn up were mostly computer-interior photos with a stray zip tie in
  frame, not a dedicated harness-building shot. The lacing photo is an honest
  substitute for the general "harness bundling/dressing" concept but is not a
  zip-tie photo.
- **Topic 7, an enclosure that is unambiguously an embedded-electronics/hobby
  project box** (vs. the building-intercom module used). Searched "project box
  open PCB", "arduino case open board", "raspberry pi case open board",
  "electronic enclosure interior standoffs board" — these mostly returned
  irrelevant academic PDFs and USACE environmental-report scans (keyword noise
  from OCR'd text), not usable photos. The Siedle intercom module is a real,
  clearly-standoff-mounted PCB inside a device enclosure and was the strongest
  verified candidate found.
