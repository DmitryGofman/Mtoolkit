All images sourced from Wikimedia Commons only. License verified via the Commons API

## enr2-shaft-collar-setscrew.svg

**What it shows:** A labeled 2D technical line-diagram (with dashed centerlines, in the
style of a machine-drawing textbook figure) of a one-piece cylindrical shaft collar
clamped onto a shaft and locked in place by a single set screw through the collar wall.
Two shaft stubs and one collar are drawn side by side. This is a schematic/diagram, not
a photo — good for explaining the *concept* of a set-screw-retained collar, though note
it depicts a solid (non-split) collar rather than a two-piece split/hinged clamping
collar (no license-clear split-hub image was found on Commons — see gaps below).

## enr2-key-keyway-keyseating.jpg

**What it shows:** A clean studio/product photo of three separate machine parts laid
out to explain "keyseating": on the left a round shaft with a machined keyway slot cut
into it, in the middle a loose rectangular parallel key, and on the right a bored hub
("mating part") with its own internal keyway — showing exactly how a key sits half in
the shaft's keyway and half in the hub's keyway to transmit torque.

**What it shows:** A top-down photo of a disassembled taper-lock bushing assembly on a
wooden bench: a steel sprocket (hub) with an internal tapered bore, its matching

## enr2-spannsatz-clamping-set.jpg

**What it shows:** A labeled 3D schematic cross-section of a "Spannsatz" (keyless
clamping/locking set) — a friction-type shaft-hub connection alternative to taper-lock
bushings: a hub (N), a shaft (W) passing through it, and a pair of mating conical rings
(K) squeezed axially by screws (S) so that the rings expand radially and clamp the hub
tightly onto the shaft purely by friction, with no key or spline needed. Directly
illustrates the "squeeze/clamp connection" principle that is the core concept of this
enrichment unit, as a second worked example alongside taper-lock.

- Original file: `File:Spannsatz-2.jpg`
- Commons page: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Spannsatz-2.jpg
- Author: User:Ukko-wc
- License: CC BY-SA 3.0
- Suggested topic: Bonus — topic 1/topic 3 (keyless friction clamping-set principle,
  a second concrete example of "צביטה"/squeeze connection alongside taper-lock)
- Type: Diagram

## Topics I could NOT find ideal images for / honest gaps

- **True two-piece/split clamping collar (the classic "split hub squeezing a shaft
  with two bolts across a slot" picture)**: searched extensively ("split shaft collar",
  "split clamp hub", "clamping hub shaft", "Klemmnabe", "geteilte Klemmnabe",
  "Spannring Welle", "Wellenklemmring") and found nothing license-clear on Commons —
  results were dominated by unrelated "collar" hits (clothing, mining shaft collars,
  archaeology finds) or non-free/irrelevant technical manuals. `enr2-shaft-collar-
  setscrew.svg` (a one-piece, non-split set-screw collar) is the closest available
  substitute; if a genuine two-piece split clamp collar photo is a hard requirement,
  it remains a gap outside Commons.
- **Keyway/key as a photo of an assembled machine** (rather than the disassembled-parts
  shot used): `enr2-key-keyway-keyseating.jpg` shows the three loose parts clearly,
  which is arguably better for teaching, but there's no clean "key seated in an
  assembled shaft-hub joint" close-up on Commons under an allowed license.
- **Taper lock / keyless bushing as a labeled cutaway diagram** (in addition to the
  photo used): only the disassembled photo (`enr2-taper-lock-hub.jpg`) was found;
  no license-clear labeled cross-section diagram of a taper-lock bushing turned up.
- **Rigid shaft coupling** (as opposed to the flexible jaw coupling used): searches for
  "rigid shaft coupling" mostly returned unrelated historical/legal documents; no
  clean rigid-coupling photo/diagram was found, so only the flexible jaw coupling
  represents topic 5.
- All six requested topics have at least one solid, precisely-matching image
  (clamp/set-screw collar, keyed shaft, taper lock, grub screw on a hub, flexible
  shaft coupling, spline shaft), plus one bonus diagram (`enr2-spannsatz-clamping-
  set.jpg`) reinforcing the keyless-clamping concept.
