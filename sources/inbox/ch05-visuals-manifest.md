## ch05-cnc-milling-coolant.jpg

**What it shows:** A close-up of a vertical CNC machining center actively cutting a
light-colored (aluminum-look) workpiece held in a machine vise. The spindle/toolholder
is visible at top, flood coolant is spraying onto the cut in a heavy white stream, and
the part sits clamped in a vise on the table. Good "machine in action" shot — you can
see the coolant hose, spindle nose, and vise jaws, but the cutting edge itself is
obscured by coolant spray (cannot confirm tool type from this photo alone).

- Original file: `File:Makino-S33-MachiningCenter-example.jpg`
- Commons page: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Makino-S33-MachiningCenter-example.jpg
- Author: Glenn McKechnie
- License: CC BY-SA 2.0
- Suggested topic: Unit intro — "CNC milling machine in action" (topic 1)
- Type: Photo

## ch05-milling-cutter-closeup.jpg

**What it shows:** An extreme close-up, shot from below, of a rotary milling cutter
mounted in a spindle, positioned just above a machine table. It is an **indexable
insert-type face/shell cutter** (visible carbide insert edges around the circumference,
red witness marks on two inserts), NOT a classic solid two/four-flute end mill — the
German title "Fingerfräser" (literally "finger mill/end mill") is the uploader's label,
but the tool geometry visible is an insert cutter. Still a strong, sharp closeup of a
rotating milling tool for teaching purposes; if precision matters, caption it as
"milling cutter with carbide inserts" rather than "end mill."

- Original file: `File:Fingerfräser.jpg`
- Commons page: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Fingerfr%C3%A4ser.jpg
- Author: Roll-Stone (de.wikipedia)
- License: Public domain
- Suggested topic: "End mill / milling cutter closeup" (topic 2)
- Type: Photo

## ch05-milling-pocket-part.jpg

**What it shows:** A dramatic close-up of a ball-nose/bull-nose milling cutter
(blurred, mid-rotation) machining a complex aluminum-colored bracket/housing part.
The part clearly shows **machined pockets, ribs, and counterbored mounting holes** —
a good real-world example of a milled part with internal features. This is a Ceratizit
(tooling manufacturer) product/application photo, so treat it as a professional
marketing shot rather than a classroom photo, but it is accurate and unstaged-looking.

## ch05-cad-vs-machined-part.jpg

**What it shows:** A two-panel comparison image: **top half** is a CAD render (gray
shaded 3D model, blue-gradient background) of an angled aluminum bracket with two
mounting flanges, a raised boss with a threaded hole, and a cluster of counterbored/
plain holes; **bottom half** is a photo of the actual CNC-machined aluminum part made
from that CAD model, same orientation. This is genuinely useful for teaching
"CAD → CNC machined part" and — as a side benefit — the bracket's rounded fillet
edge (visible top-left, a curved transition face) is a real example of a rounded
feature left by a round cutting tool, though it is not a dedicated corner-radius
diagram. Re-saved as JPEG (quality 82, resized to 1000×1290) to fit the ~400KB budget;
original was a 1.4MB PNG.

- Original file: `File:CAD model and CNC machined part.PNG`
- Commons page: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:CAD_model_and_CNC_machined_part.PNG
- Author: Mike1024
- License: Public domain
- Suggested topic: "Machined aluminium part with pockets" (topic 6, primary choice —
  also the closest thing I found to topic 4, internal corner radius, see note below)
- Type: Photo + CAD render composite (photo half needs no light plate; if the CAD-render
  half is cropped out for use as a line diagram it would need a light plate)

## ch05-climb-milling-diagram.png

**What it shows:** A simple 2-color line diagram (orange workpiece block, light-blue
cutter cross-section) illustrating **climb milling**: cutter rotation arrow, material
feed direction arrow (downward), a labeled "chip" forming at the top where the tool
first engages, and "cutting depth (feed per tooth)" dimension line. Clean, simple,
directly usable as a teaching diagram — pairs with the conventional-milling diagram
below (same author, same style, meant to be shown side by side).

- Original file: `File:Climb Milling 01.png`
- Commons page: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Climb_Milling_01.png
- Author: Rocketmagnet
- License: Public domain
- Suggested topic: "Milling process diagram — climb milling" (topic 7)
- Type: Diagram (flat vector-style colors, no light plate needed — already clean
  white background)

## ch05-conventional-milling-diagram.png

**What it shows:** Same visual style/pair as the climb-milling diagram above, this
time illustrating **conventional (up-cut) milling**: cutter rotation arrow, material
feed arrow (upward relative to cutter engagement), cutting-depth dimension line, and a
label "material to be removed by the next tooth" pointing at the thin, tapering chip
that starts near zero thickness — the defining visual difference from climb milling.

- Original file: `File:Conventional Milling 01.png`
- Commons page: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Conventional_Milling_01.png
- Author: Rocketmagnet
- License: Public domain
- Suggested topic: "Milling process diagram — conventional milling" (topic 7, paired
  with climb-milling diagram)
- Type: Diagram (flat vector-style colors, no light plate needed)
