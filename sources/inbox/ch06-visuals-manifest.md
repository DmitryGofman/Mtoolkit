# Chapter 6 visuals manifest — motor selection & motion (בחירת מנועים ותנועה)

All images sourced from Wikimedia Commons, license verified via the Commons API
(`action=query&prop=imageinfo&iiprop=extmetadata`). Saved to
`app/public/visuals/` with the `ch06-` prefix. Existing reusable asset:
`racing-drone.jpg` (already in the repo) shows BLDC motors mounted on a drone —
usable as a second BLDC illustration alongside the outrunner closeup below.

## 1. ch06-brushed-dc-motor-disassembled.jpg

- **Shows (precisely):** A small brushed DC motor taken apart into its three
  main sub-assemblies, laid side by side: the end-cap with brush contacts and
  bearing (left), the rotor/armature with copper-wound core and commutator
  segments on a shaft (center), and the permanent-magnet outer can/housing
  (right).
- **Commons page:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Brushed_DC_motor_disassembled.jpg
- **Author:** Base photo by Ilia Krivoruk ("Brushed_dc_motor_assembly.jpg"); this cropped/composited derivative by Andy Dingley
- **License:** CC BY-SA 3.0
- **Suggested topic:** 1 — Brushed DC motor (construction/parts)
- **Type:** photo

## 2. ch06-dc-motor-cutaway-photo.jpg

- **Shows (precisely):** Close-up photo of a mid-size industrial DC motor with
  its steel housing partially removed/open, exposing the field winding coils
  (tan/orange fabric-wrapped interpole and main-field windings), the
  commutator, brush holders and brush leads, and the armature/rotor shaft with
  bearing. Original Commons title calls out "interpole and main field winding
  as well as commutator." Downscaled and re-encoded from the ~9.9 MB original
  PNG to a ~78 KB JPEG for repo size budget (image content unchanged, just
  compressed).
- **Commons page:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:DC_motor_with_interpole_and_main_field_winding_as_well_as_commutator.png
- **Author:** Oliver Wallscheid
- **License:** CC BY 4.0
- **Suggested topic:** 7 — Electric motor cutaway/anatomy (rotor, stator/field windings, brushes, commutator)
- **Type:** photo

## 3. ch06-outrunner-bldc.jpg

- **Shows (precisely):** A brushless DC (BLDC) "outrunner" motor as used on
  small drones/RC aircraft, seen from the top with a propeller mounted on the
  shaft. The photo exposes the stator with copper coil windings around teeth,
  inside the rotating magnet bell/can. NOTE: the image itself has an author-added
  English text overlay explaining "Outrunner DC Brushless motor... magnets are
  the rectangles... entire housing spins... three wires connected to the
  coils... controlled by an ESC" — the overlay is part of the source file, not
  something we added.
- **Commons page:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Outrunner.jpg
- **Author:** 1sfoerster
- **License:** CC BY-SA 3.0
- **Suggested topic:** 2 — Brushless (BLDC) outrunner motor
- **Type:** photo (with embedded caption text — flag for captioner since the image already contains English words)

## 7. ch06-spur-gear-train.jpg

- **Shows (precisely):** A cutaway/sectioned demonstration model of a
  three-stage helical spur gear reduction gearbox, painted red with a yellow
  wall-thickness highlight coat (typical museum/teaching cutaway convention).
  Shows the meshing helical gear teeth of three gear stages stacked on
  parallel shafts inside the housing, with visible bearings and shaft seals.
  German title: "Aufgeschnittenes dreistufiges Stirnradgetriebe mit schräger
  Verzahnung" (sectioned three-stage spur gearbox with helical teeth).
- **Commons page:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Stirnradgetriebe_dreistufig.jpg
- **Author:** Lüt
- **License:** CC BY-SA 3.0
- **Suggested topic:** 5 — Gearbox / gear train (real hardware cutaway, complements the planetary diagram above)
- **Type:** photo
