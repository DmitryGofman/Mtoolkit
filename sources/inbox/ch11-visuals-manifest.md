## ch11-welding-symbol-elements.png

**What it shows:** A clean labeled line diagram (AWS-style) of a complete welding
symbol reference line, showing every standard element and its position: arrow,
reference line, basic weld symbol / detail reference, tail, finish symbol, contour
symbol, groove angle, root opening, effective throat, length/pitch of weld,
field-weld symbol, weld-all-around symbol, and the "arrow side / other side / both
sides" convention. A comprehensive teaching diagram for reading welding symbols on
drawings.

- Author: Milton H Hamilton, Gordon R. Sullivan
- License: Public domain
- Topic: 6 — Welding symbols on a drawing (diagram)
- Type: Diagram (original PNG, 60.7 KB, no re-encoding needed)

## ch11-welding-distortion-diagram.svg

**What it shows:** A clean vector diagram illustrating angular distortion caused by
welding: two views of a plate/section pair, shown as flat before welding (dashed
outline, labeled "ORIGINAL SIZE") and bent/warped after a V-groove weld is made
down the middle, with the resulting angular deformation visible at the joint.
Directly illustrates the "welding causes shrinkage/distortion" concept.

## Topics I could NOT find a strong dedicated image for / gaps

- **Topic 4, a photo showing classic porosity (pinhole gas pores) or undercut in
  isolation.** Searched "weld defect", "porosity weld", "bad weld", "weld
  undercut", "weld porosity photo", "welding porosity slag inclusion", "poor
  quality weld". Commons has good *diagrams* of these defects (see rejected
  candidates below) but real close-up photographs specifically of porosity
  pinholes are scarce and what surfaced were mostly academic PDF scans (OCR
  keyword matches), not standalone photos. `ch11-bad-weld-porosity.jpg` (uneven
  bead, poor workmanship) is an honest, verified substitute for "bad weld" in
  general, but is not a porosity-specific close-up.
  - Rejected candidate: `File:Weld Undercut.jpg` (CC BY-SA 4.0) — captioned
    "Undercut created during welding" but the photo itself is ambiguous (a white
    painted/composite panel with a wrinkle-like defect); I could not visually
    confirm it as a genuine metal weld undercut, so I left it out rather than
    risk mislabeling.
  - Two clean defect *diagrams* were found and could be added as bonus/companion
    images if desired: `File:UNDERCUT.png` (CC BY-SA 4.0, labeled cross-section of
    a weld undercut) and `File:Cracks.jpg` (CC0, labeled diagram of longitudinal/
    transverse weld cracks) — not downloaded, since the manifest already covers
    topic 4, but both passed the license check and are available if a second
    defect image is wanted.
- **Topic 7, a real photograph of visible welding warping/distortion** (rather
  than a diagram). Searched "welding distortion diagram", "weld shrinkage
  warping", German "Schweißverzug" (no hits). Only clean vector diagrams turned
  up (used `ch11-welding-distortion-diagram.svg`); no verified real-world "before
  and after" warped-part photo was found in this pass.
- **Topic 6, alternative:** only one strong welding-symbol diagram was pursued
  (`Elements of a welding symbol.PNG`); Commons also has several French-language
  symbol diagrams (`Symboles soudure angle.svg`, `Symboles soudure V.svg`, CC
  licensed) in `Category:Welding symbols` if a French/simpler visual variant is
  ever preferred.
